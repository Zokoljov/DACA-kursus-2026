## Cheat Sheet: How to Write for Each Person

### 🎯 Kristi Tamm — CEO (ENTJ)
- **Lead with the conclusion**, not the method.
- Strategic framing: "What does this mean for growth?"
- Visuals over text. Numbers *in context* ("+20% vs last quarter").
- Always pair a problem with a recommendation.
- **Avoid:** hedging ("maybe", "not sure"), technical detail, slow timelines.
- **Signature line:** *"Mida ma peaksin selle info põhjal tegema?"* (What should I do with this?)

### 🔧 Toomas Kask — IT Director (ISTJ)
- Formal tone, precise terminology (PostgreSQL, not "the database").
- Full documentation: every step, every query, audit logs.
- Show your QA: "Checked for NULLs, duplicates, validated dates."
- Justify your choices ("I chose this approach because...").
- **Avoid:** "AI did it" without validation, casual tone, skipped logging.
- **Signature line:** *"Kus on andmepuhastuse audit log?"* (Where's the cleaning audit log?)

### 📣 Anna Mets — Head of Marketing
- Visual, colorful, fast. Charts > tables > raw numbers.
- Tell a story: "Customers who buy sustainable products also buy premium..."
- Quick turnaround expected; casual tone (emojis fine) but still professional.
- **Avoid:** long technical explanations, gray unformatted tables, multi-day delays.
- **Signature line:** *"Kas saaksid teha selle graafikuna?"* (Can you make that a chart?)

### 🔍 Marko Saar — Product Manager (INTP)
- Wants methodology, not just results: "How did you calculate this?"
- Welcomes follow-up questions and being challenged — engage, don't just assert.
- Detail-oriented; cite data sources explicitly.
- **Avoid:** vague answers, "just trust it," skipping the "why."
- **Signature line:** *"Huvitav! Aga kuidas sa seda arvutasid?"* (Interesting! But how did you calculate that?)

### 📦 Liis Koppel — Operations Manager (ESFJ)
- Plain language, no jargon ("koondatud andmed", not "aggregated data").
- Practical and actionable: "What should we do *today*?"
- Store-level specificity (Tallinn vs. Tartu vs. Pärnu), not abstract trends.
- **Avoid:** theory without application, ignoring whether store staff can use it.
- **Signature line:** *"Aga kuidas me seda reaalses elus kasutame?"* (But how do we use this in real life?)

## The Underlying Tension (Useful for Reflection/Retrospective)

- **Kristi vs. Toomas:** speed vs. thoroughness
- **Anna vs. Toomas:** visual/fast vs. documented/formal
- **Marko vs. Anna:** depth vs. speed
- **Liis vs. Kristi:** team capacity vs. aggressive growth

Same analysis, five different audiences — the program's real lesson is learning to reshape one finding for each stakeholder.
